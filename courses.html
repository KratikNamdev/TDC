Diploma in Hospitality Management

Diploma in Hospitality Management: Why this particular course?
The course can be completed in a short duration and has the potential to get great packages and become a professional depending upon the student’s skill set and advancement in the industry.  Students pursuing the course inculcate quality organizational skills, leadership, and teamwork which helps them to improve their communication and interpersonal skills for the long-term goal.  Student’s become quality-oriented and learn to deal with criticism.

Human Resource Management: Concept and functions OF HRM, objectives, HRM in tourism in industry, human resource planning, training and development, recruitment and selection, training and development.

Tourism Management: Concept and functions of management, role, skills and tasks of manager, concept and theories for motivation of tourism, leadership theories and styles, individual and group behavior.

Travel Management: Objectives, concept of transport, linkages between transport and tourism, characteristics of tourist transpiration, means of transport in India, transport to promote tourist.

Itinerary Planning and Tour Packaging; Objectives, meaning and types of itinerary, steps in developing/ planning a tour itinerary, background information for preparing itinerary, itinerary for git and Fit tours, meaning and types  of tour packages, package elements and deals, Group inclusive tours and tailor- made package or Fits, package tour formulation process, tour brochure  designing, tour voucher.          

Communication and Personality: Objectives, concept and meaning communication, oral communication, meetings, written communication, principles of effective writing, customer care management, personality.

Hospitality and Catering Management: Objectives, Hospitality management, components of tourism industry. factors impacting the hospitality, classification of hotels, Guest expectations during arrival departure, various departments of a hotel, different types   cuisines, various departments of a hotel, different types of cuisines.

6 months classroom & practical training

Artisan Barista Certification

This comprehensive Barista Training Course is designed to provide participants with the knowledge and skills needed to excel as a professional barista. The course covers everything from the selection and sourcing of coffee beans, to the art and science of coffee brewing and presentation. COURSE OUTLINE


Module 1: Introduction to Coffee

  • History and origins of coffee
  • Coffee seed lifecycle and impact of geography on it
  • Different types of coffee beans and their characteristics
  • Coffee grading and cupping
  • Coffee flavour wheel

Module 2: Coffee Brewing Techniques

  • Coffee fundamentals (proportion, grind, water, freshness)
  • Espresso basics and extraction principles
  • Different brewing methods - espresso machine brew - manual brew (e.g., pour over, French press, etc.)
  • Temperature, pressure, and flow rate control

Module 3: Coffee Presentation and Latte Art

  • Milk steaming and texturing
  • Milk pouring techniques
  • Different types of latte art
  • Understanding Milk, froth, espresso & water proportion for creating Different coffees
  • Basic coffee menu and drink preparation

Module 4: Customer Service and Professionalism

  • Communication and customer service skills
  • Health and safety regulations
  • Basic barista equipment maintenance and cleaning

Module 5: Hands-On Practical Training

  • Participants will have the opportunity to practice and hone their skills under the guidance of experienced baristas
  • Participants will be able to brew coffee, prepare and serve espresso drinks, and create latte art designs
  • The course will be taught by experienced and certified Barista trainers and will include hands-on training in a professional coffee shop environment. Upon completion of the course, participants will receive a certificate of completion and will be well-prepared to start their career as a barista.

Duration: The course will be run for a duration of 6 weeks, with classes held 2 hours twice in a week. For remaining 4 days candidate is expected to work in a café for live practical training.
